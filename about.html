Fiona Apple

Fiona Apple was born in New York City with her father being an actor and her mother being a muician. By the age of 8, Apple was trained on the piano and began composing her own pieces. As a child, she dealt with many struggles and survived a very serious assault that evolved into ptsd, depression, and an eating disorder. She is a big advocate of justice in the criminal court system as well as animal welfare. Apple's poem "When the Pawn Hits the Conflict" is a response to her experience in the music industry.
